: Berlin’s Unique Shape: A Dog's Head from Above :

A fun fact about Berlin is that its layout resembles a dog’s head when viewed from above. The shape is created by the winding River Spree, which outlines the "head," while various neighborhoods and parks form the ears and eyes. The Ringbahn, a circular railway line that encircles much of the inner city, naturally creates the whole outer shape. This interesting resemblance adds a quirky layer to the city’s character and reflects its unique urban planning.
